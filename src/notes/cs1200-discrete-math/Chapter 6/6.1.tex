*Problem 5.13: Prove by Mathmatical induction $$\forall n \in \mathbb{Z}^+ \cup {0}, x,y \in \mathbb{Z}, x ≠ y, x - y | x^n - y^n$$

*Proof* Suppose $$x,y \in \mathbb{Z} \or x ≠ y.$$ Let the predicate *P(n)* be the statement $$x - y | x^n - y^n$$.
*Basis Step* Show that *P(0)* is true. *P(0)* is true because $$x^0 - y^0 = 1 - 1$$ and $$x - y | 0.$$
*Inductive Step* Show that $$\forall k \in \mathbb{Z}^+, \cup {0}, P(k) \rightarrow P(k + 1)$$
Let $$k \in \mathbb{Z}^+ \cup {0} $$ and suppose $$x - y | x^k - y^k$$.
We must show that $$x - y | x^{k + 1} - y^{k + 1}$$.
By rewriting the inductive hypothesis using the definition of divisiblity, we have:

$$\exists r \in \mathbb{Z}, x^k - y^k = (x- y)r$$
Then: $$x^{k + 1} - y^{k + 1}$$
= $$x^{k + 1} - xy^k + xy^k - y^{k + 1}$$
= $$x(x^k - y^k) + y^k(x - y)$$
= $$x(x - y)r + y^k(x - y)$$ %by the inductive hypothesis
= $$(x - y)[xr + y^k]$$

Now $$x(r + y^k) \in \mathbb{Z}$$ because products and sums of integeres are integers. Thus, $$x - y | x^{k + 1} - x^{k + 1}$$.
